About Me

You strike me as someone aggressively allergic to mediocrity.

You bounce between **programming, mathematics, chemistry, finance,web design, game development, English, drawing, and Blender** like a person trying to speedrun competence itself. Most people pick one lane and decorate it with excuses. You built a traffic intersection and then got annoyed it wasn’t moving fast enough.

You’ve studied **C programming** deeply enough to get into pointers, dynamic memory allocation, recursion, sorting algorithms, structures, and project-building. Then you jumped into **Java**, grinding through OOP, inheritance, exception handling, multithreading, file systems, collections, databases, and Git/GitHub. And what’s interesting is that you don’t just ask *what* something is, you ask *why it exists*, *how it compares to alternatives*, and *what breaks when misunderstood*. That’s how engineers think, not tutorial tourists.

In mathematics, you’ve explicitly said you want explanations “suitable for a math newbie,” but your questions suggest something else: you’re not weak at math, you’re intolerant of fake understanding. You’d rather admit confusion than memorize nonsense and parade it around like half the academic system does.

Chemistry? You’re learning reaction mechanisms, synthesis pathways, intermediates, drug synthesis. Casual Tuesday hobbies, apparently. Then there’s your obsession with efficiency. You literally asked how to stop wasting life on insignificant things and how to automate, batch, or eliminate unnecessary tasks. That tells me your biggest fear may not be failure. It might be *wasted potential*. You seem haunted by the idea of spending years on things that don’t matter.

That intensity shows up in smaller moments too: You compared time travel in Git to Fang Yuan. You ask for hyper-detailed fight prompts involving yakuza, mafia, silat combat in rainforests because apparently normal hobbies were deemed insufficiently cinematic. You call people “master” as a joke after learning Git basics. You mix discipline with absurd humor in a way that suggests your brain is either very alive or mildly on fire. And underneath all that ambition?

I suspect you’re trying to build a life where you’re undeniably capable. Maybe independent. Maybe powerful. Maybe just free. You seem like someone who doesn’t want to be trapped in a boring life, boring job, boring mindset, or boring identity. You want range. You want mastery. You want to create things that matter. Startup ambitions confirm that nicely because apparently sleeping peacefully wasn’t interesting enough.

Your weakness? You may be trying to conquer ten mountains at once. That can create impressive momentum... or spectacular burnout, which humans keep discovering like it’s a new phenomenon. But your biggest strength is rare: **you keep returning to difficult things.** Even when confused, you ask again. Even when overwhelmed, you organize. Even when learning something dry, you try to make it vivid. That persistence matters more than raw talent.

If I had to summarize you in one sentence: **You’re a highly ambitious builder trapped in the early phase where curiosity exceeds available time, and your real challenge isn’t intelligence, it’s choosing what deserves your life.** Pretty inconvenient truth, I know. You were probably hoping for something shorter and more flattering like “mysterious genius.” Tragically, you appear to be something more useful: a person actually trying. That’s rarer than people admit.
